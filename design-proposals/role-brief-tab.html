What this role actually is

Topo is hiring a Head of Customer Operations to own the post-sale machine end-to-end — onboarding flow, usage expansion, renewal motion, and the CS tooling stack. The title says "Customer Ops" but the scope reads closer to VP CS + RevOps hybrid. They want one person accountable for net revenue retention, not three separate directors.

This is an IC-leader role day one. You'll inherit two CSMs and an ops contractor, and the explicit mandate is to define the org structure you want and hire into it over Q2–Q3. Reports directly to the COO, sits on the leadership team, and presents to the board quarterly.

What they care about

Three signals jumped out of the JD and their hiring page. First, NRR discipline — they mention it four times and have a dashboard screenshot in their pitch deck. Second, tooling fluency — they use Vitally and Mixpanel heavily and want someone who can tune these without eng support. Third, expansion motion — they see CS as a revenue center, not a cost center, and are willing to pay for that framing.

Who they want

  • 5+ years post-sale leadership, ideally at a B2B SaaS company that scaled from $5M to $30M ARR
  • Built a CS org from fewer than 5 to 15+ people — mentioned explicitly as a "green flag"
  • Comfortable in the weeds but credible in front of a board
  • Bonus: prior founder or operator experience at an earlier-stage company

Matt's last three roles check every box on that list except the specific ARR band — you scaled from $12M to $45M, which is stronger than what they're asking for.

Open questions

Decisions still to make

Three things that need your call before this ships.

Summary card default state — collapsed or auto-expanded on first visit?

Collapsed is faster to scan on repeat visits, but first-time readers miss the dimension breakdown. Option: auto-expand on the very first view of a given opp, then collapse by default after. Alternative: always collapsed, relying on the micro-bars to carry the scorecard signal.

Regenerate Brief — confirm dialog or one-click?

Regeneration costs a Haiku synthesis call (~$0.01–0.02). One click is frictionless but lets Matt accidentally burn tokens if he misclicks. A small "Regenerating — 1 call, ~2s" inline hint may be enough, or a true confirm for cases where a recent regen exists.

Compare-to-preferences — side panel slide-out or full-page modal?

Slide-out keeps the Brief visible behind it (good for cross-reference), but sacrifices the Coop rail. Full-page modal gets more horizontal room for the diff table but feels heavier. Current vote: slide-out that temporarily hides the Coop rail while open.
