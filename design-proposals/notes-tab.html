Open questions

Everything above is a specific stance. These three items I didn't decide unilaterally.

Q1
Markdown-only, or also WYSIWYG toggle?
Pure-markdown is the Granola / Notion-lite / Obsidian paradigm — fastest for power users, mildly intimidating for everyone else. A WYSIWYG toggle adds surface area and a small decision every session. Lean: markdown-only with inline live rendering (what's shown above). The live rendering already hides the syntax visually — raw md only appears when editing that line.
Q2
Coop's notes: always visible, or hidden until Coop has something?
Always-visible means an empty "Coop's notes" section sits under every opp, which is a dead zone on new saves. Hidden-until-populated is cleaner but means the user has to learn the section exists. Lean: hidden until populated, with a subtle auto-scroll when the first Coop entry lands so the user sees it happen.
Q3
Long doc: TOC sidebar at some length threshold, or never?
If notes stay under ~500 words most of the time, a TOC is over-engineering. If they grow to several screens (likely on an active opp over months), a floating TOC pulled from H2 headings is genuinely useful. Lean: auto-reveal at 3+ H2 headings, docked on the right edge of the notes panel, collapses when user scrolls past the last heading.
