Identity bar on top, chat input on bottom — constant in every state. Between them, a persistent detection chip declares what the panel thinks the current context is, with a Show pipeline escape hatch. The content block under the chip swaps per state. Why not B or C

Two alternatives considered and rejected. Kept tight — this isn't the hero.

Option B — Radically different layouts per state
Rejected
Each state would be purpose-built — a single-CTA takeover for Not Saved, a stats-heavy dashboard for Global, a dense research dump when Saved. Rejected because transitions between states feel jarring, users lose spatial anchoring, and the panel turns into a pile of different apps. DESIGN.md rule 4 (one treatment) argues against it.
Option C — Silent swap, no detection chip
Rejected
Minimal chrome — content just swaps underneath without any indicator of what state the panel is in. Rejected because it re-creates the #243 bug: user can't tell the panel guessed wrong (or guessed right) until they read the body. Detection must be surfaced as a label, not implied by content.

Open questions — resolved

Three items Matt verdicted in the workshop.

✅ User override — is Show pipeline enough?
Matt: enough for now. We ship with Show pipeline as the manual escape and hold off on per-domain blocklist until a repeat offender emerges in real use.
✅ Loading states — what does the chip say mid-detection?
Matt: approved the "Checking…" shimmer idea. The chip dot pulses mid-detection; content area holds the prior state visible to avoid a jarring blank. Resolve or fall back to Pipeline Dashboard after ~1.5s timeout.
✅ Transition motion — fade or swap?
Matt: crossfade approved. Content block fades out at var(--motion-sm) ease-out, new block fades in at the same duration. Matches the tab-switch cadence in design tokens. No hard swaps — they feel cheap and cause motion sickness on fast nav.
