The problem

A job search is a stack of manual workflows that don't talk to each other.

Every active opportunity ends up scattered across half a dozen tools. The pipeline stops being current the moment things start moving, and the AI you'd ask for help has to be re-briefed on your life every single time.

Manual research, every time

A new company means opening tabs across LinkedIn, Glassdoor, and the company site, trying to piece together what they do and whether they're worth pursuing. Repeat for every opportunity.

Spreadsheets that go stale

An Excel sheet with stages, next steps, and key players works as a snapshot and breaks down the moment things start moving. Impossible to keep current across dozens of active opportunities.

Bookmark tools with no context

Extensions that save job postings to apply to later, but with no research, no relationship history, and no pipeline management attached.

ChatGPT with no memory

Pasting Granola transcripts, screenshotting email threads, re-explaining your background — just to ask "what should I say next." The AI never has the full picture and you rebuild context every conversation.

Free-form application questions

Every application asks "why are you interested in this role." Answering well means synthesizing your story, the company, and why the role connects them — hours of work per application with no leverage.

Qualification guesswork

Trying to assess whether a company and role are a fit before investing time, based on a job description that's always an incomplete picture of the actual role.

The solution

One surface where research, pipeline, and context live together.

Visit a company page and the work happens automatically. Save it and it joins a pipeline that stays current. Ask Coop a question and it already knows the company, the role, your background, and the email and meeting history.

Instant research

Full company profile, no manual entry

Auto-detects the company from any LinkedIn, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, Ashby, or generic site. Pulls firmographics from Apollo, leadership and reviews from web search, and synthesizes a structured profile with Claude. Cached for 24 hours so you don't burn calls re-researching.

Pipeline management

Kanban that stays current

Saved companies and opportunities live as one record in a single pipeline. Drag through customizable stages, filter by stage or owner, track who the ball is on with my-court / their-court. Stage timestamps record themselves.

Coop, the advisor

An AI that already has the context

Coop chats with the full picture: your professional profile, the company research, the job description, fit scoring, plus every email thread, calendar event, and Granola meeting transcript tied to that company. You ask the question instead of spending twenty minutes rebuilding context.

Job fit scoring

1–10 scoring against your real preferences

Scores any job description against your roles, salary floors, work arrangement, and resume — modified by your own excitement rating so the verdict reflects how you actually feel, not just what the JD says.

How it works

Detect, research, pipeline.

Three steps. The first two happen on a single click each. The third is where the work actually pays off.

STEP 01

Detect

The content script reads the page — LinkedIn profile, ATS posting, company site — and identifies the company and (when present) the job posting. Open the side panel and you see what was detected.

STEP 02

Research

Click research and the pipeline runs: Apollo for firmographics, Serper for reviews and leadership and product, Claude to synthesize a clean structured profile. Job descriptions get scored against your preferences.

STEP 03

Pipeline

Save it and it joins the Kanban. Gmail and Calendar attach relevant threads and meetings by company domain. Granola attaches meeting transcripts by participant. Coop is one click away with all of it loaded.

Under the hood

Local-first, no backend, no build step.

Coop.ai is a Manifest V3 Chrome extension. There is no server. There is no account. There is no build pipeline. All data lives in chrome.storage.local on your machine. API keys are stored locally and entered once through the in-extension Integrations page.

A service worker mediates every external call. The UI surfaces — content script, side panel, saved dashboard, company detail view, opportunity detail view — communicate with it through message passing. All side effects route through one place, which keeps the surface area small enough that one person can hold the whole thing in their head.

Chat is fault-tolerant by design. The model fallback chain cycles providers automatically when one fails:

GPT-4.1 mini  →  Claude Haiku  →  Claude Sonnet  →  GPT-4.1
