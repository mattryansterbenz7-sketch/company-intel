# Coop.ai — 90-Second Demo Script

Target length: 90 seconds. Tight. No re-takes mid-flow — record the click path once, narrate over it.

## Cold open (t = 0)

On screen: a LinkedIn job posting, full browser, no extension UI visible. Pick a real one for a company you've already had a meeting or email exchange with — that's what makes the payoff land.

---

## Beat-by-beat

**0:00 – 0:08 — The hook**
Action: just sit on the LinkedIn job page.
VO: "Every job search runs on the same broken stack. Six tabs, a spreadsheet, and a ChatGPT window where you re-paste your resume every time. I built one thing that replaces all of it."

**0:08 – 0:18 — Open sidepanel, detection**
Action: click the extension icon to open the sidepanel. The sidepanel detects the company and shows the cached or empty state.
VO: "Sidepanel detects the company from the page. Nothing has fired yet — no API calls on open. It waits for me."

**0:18 – 0:30 — Research click**
Action: click the **Research** button. Show the loading state, then the populated profile — employees, funding, leadership, reviews, hiring signals.
VO: "One click pulls firmographics from Apollo, leadership and reviews from web search, and Claude synthesizes the whole thing. Cached for a day."

**0:30 – 0:38 — Save as opportunity**
Action: click **Save as Opportunity**. Score appears.
VO: "Save it as an opportunity. The job description gets scored against my profile, my preferred roles, my floors. One to ten, with the reasoning."

**0:38 – 0:55 — Coop chat with real context**
Action: scroll to the chat panel in the sidepanel, which is now bound to this opportunity (show the bound-entry pill in the header). Type or paste: "What did we already talk about with these folks, and what should I lead with in the application?"
Let Coop answer. The answer references actual meeting notes or email threads.
VO: "Coop is already bound to this opportunity. He has the company data, the job description, my profile, my emails with anyone here, and my Granola meeting notes — all loaded. So I can ask the question instead of spending twenty minutes rebuilding context."

**0:55 – 1:08 — Coop Assist in Gmail**
Action: cut to a Gmail compose window addressed to a contact at the same company. Type out: "Hi Priya, I hope this finds you well. I wanted to circle back on the Staff PMM role and leverage our last call…" Three coral underlines bloom under the flagged phrases. The "Coop · 3 ideas" pill slides in by the field. Click the pill, panel opens, click **In my voice**. The whole field rewrites to a direct, specific version that closes with "—Matt".
VO: "Coop doesn't just live in the side panel. He's ambient. Anywhere I'm typing — Gmail, LinkedIn, an application form — he runs local heuristics against my voice profile. No 'circle back,' no 'hope this finds you well.' One click rewrites the whole thing in my voice."
Reaction line: "That sounded like a person."

**1:08 – 1:22 — Apply Queue swipe**
Action: navigate to the Apply Queue. Swipe through two or three cards — pass on one, mark another interested, click Open Application on a third.
VO: "Apply Queue is the swipe surface for everything still unscored. Pass, interested, or open the application — and when I open it, Coop in the sidepanel auto-binds to that opportunity so the form already has its advisor."

**1:22 – 1:30 — Close**
Action: cut back to the kanban dashboard.
VO: "Built it for myself. Local, no backend, my keys, my data. DM me if you want a walkthrough."
