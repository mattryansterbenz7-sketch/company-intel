Overview

CompanyIntel is a Chrome extension that turns your browser into a lightweight CRM. It researches companies as you browse, tracks your relationships and outreach, and connects your Gmail and meeting notes to give you context exactly when you need it.

Instant Company Research

Visit any company website or LinkedIn page and the sidepanel automatically pulls firmographics, leadership, funding, reviews, and hiring signals — no manual input required.

Save to CRM

One click saves a company or opportunity to your personal pipeline. Data syncs across the sidepanel and the full-screen company view.

Activity Tracking New

Email threads from Gmail and Granola meeting notes surface automatically in the company view so you always know where a relationship stands.

Known Contacts New

The extension detects people from a company's domain in your emails and saves them as verified contacts, separate from API-guessed leadership.

Opportunity Pipeline

Tag any company as an opportunity and track it through a custom Kanban pipeline — from first contact to offer stage.

Job Match Scoring

When browsing a job posting, CompanyIntel scores the role against your preferences and background using Claude AI.

AI Chat New

A contextual chat panel on every company and opportunity page. Includes full email history, Granola meeting transcripts, known contacts, reviews, and a timeline-aware system prompt.

Pipeline Celebrations New

Confetti animations and sounds fire when dragging opportunities to key stages. Type (emoji vs classic confetti) and sound are fully configurable per stage in Settings.

Sidepanel

The sidepanel opens automatically when you browse a company website or LinkedIn page. It's your research-on-demand layer — no tab switching required.

How research works

1
Company detected
The content script reads the page URL and title to identify the company. For LinkedIn company pages, it extracts the company name and slug directly.
2
Research runs in background
Apollo.io returns firmographics (employees, funding, founded year, industry). Serper runs Google searches for leadership, reviews, and job listings. Claude AI synthesizes everything into a structured company profile.
3
Results cached
Research is cached in chrome.storage.local keyed by company name. Subsequent visits return instantly. Cache is used as-is; hit Refresh to re-run research.
4
Save to pipeline
Click Save Company to add to your CRM. If you're on a job posting, Save as Opportunity captures the role and runs a Job Match score against your preferences.

Sidepanel sections

  • Company Overview — employees, funding, industry, founded year, one-liner, category
  • Hiring Signals — open roles found on LinkedIn Jobs, Greenhouse, and Lever
  • Known Contacts — people from the company's domain found in your Gmail (appears once contacts are detected)
  • Leadership — founders and executives sourced from LinkedIn and news results
  • Reviews & Signal — Glassdoor, Blind, RepVue, and Reddit snippets
💡 The View in CRM link appears after you save a company. It opens the full-screen company view for that entry.
Company View

The full-screen company view (company.html) is your CRM record for a company. It has three columns — a left properties sidebar, a main tabbed content area, and a right context sidebar.

Layout — company.html
Left Sidebar
Properties — website, LinkedIn, employees, funding, founded, industry + custom fields
Tags — freeform labels
Main — Hub Tabs
Intel (default) — company overview, intel, employee reviews
Notes — free-form notes, auto-saved on blur
Emails — Gmail threads related to this company, lazy-loaded on first tab click
Meetings — Granola meeting notes, lazy-loaded on first tab click
Right Sidebar
Known Contacts — verified via email/calendar
Leadership — API-sourced, email shown if contact matched
Opportunity — pipeline stage, role, job posting link
Hiring Signals — open roles filtered to this company

Header

  • Company Status — dropdown to set where this company sits in your company pipeline (Watch List → Archived)
  • Star Rating — 1–5 stars, used for filtering in Saved view
  • Offer Stage → — if tagged as an opportunity, shows current opportunity stage and jumps to the Opportunity panel when clicked
  • + Add to Pipeline — appears when not yet an opportunity; one click adds to the Opportunity pipeline at "Needs Review"

Panel drag-and-drop

Panels in the left and right sidebars can be dragged to reorder or moved between columns. Layout is saved per-company in localStorage. Collapse any panel with the ▲ button in its header.

ℹ The main column (hub tabs) is not drag-and-drop configurable. Its content is fixed: Intel, Notes, Emails, Meetings.
Opportunity Pipeline

Any saved company can be tagged as an Opportunity. Opportunities appear in their own Kanban pipeline in the Saved view, tracked separately from your general company research.

Architecture

Opportunities are not separate records — they are companies with isOpportunity: true set. This means a company record and its opportunity share a single entry, eliminating the sync issues that come with duplicated data.

Opportunity-specific fields are stored directly on the company entry:

  • isOpportunity — boolean flag
  • jobStage — current pipeline stage (separate from status which tracks company research stage)
  • jobTitle — role title
  • jobUrl — link to the original job posting
  • jobMatch — AI match score and analysis
  • jobSnapshot — salary, work arrangement, employment type

Default pipeline stages

Needs Review Want to Apply Applied Intro Requested Conversations in Progress Offer Stage Accepted Rejected / DQ'd

Stages are fully customizable in Setup. Custom stages persist in chrome.storage.local as opportunityStages.

Job Match scoring

When you save a job posting from the sidepanel, Claude AI scores the role (1–10) against your background, preferred roles, work arrangement, and salary floor set in Preferences. The score and a breakdown (strong fits, red flags, verdict) are saved on the entry and shown on Kanban cards.

Job snapshot — Perks vs Salary

When a job posting is saved, LinkedIn chips are scanned and classified: amounts matching salary patterns ($120K, $120,000) go to Base Salary; chip text containing words like stipend, allowance, reimbursement, or subsidy is classified as a Perk instead. Perks appear as teal chips below the salary row so items like "$50/mo internet stipend" are never confused with base compensation.

Emails

The Emails tab in the company view shows Gmail threads related to the company, lazy-loaded the first time you click the tab.

How emails are fetched

1
Query built
A Gmail search query is constructed using the company domain (from:*@domain.com OR to:*@domain.com), company name, and LinkedIn slug. Up to 20 messages are fetched.
2
Full message bodies fetched
Each message is fetched with format=full to get the complete body. MIME parts are parsed recursively to find text/plain content.
3
Quoted content stripped
Reply chains, >-quoted lines, and On [date]... wrote: headers are stripped so each message shows only its new content.
4
Threads grouped, contacts extracted
Messages are grouped by threadId. Any sender/recipient whose email domain matches the company domain is saved as a Known Contact on the entry.

Thread UI

  • Each thread shows as a collapsible card: subject, participants, date, message count
  • Click a thread header to expand/collapse all messages in that thread
  • Within a thread, each individual message can be expanded by clicking its sender row — the latest message starts expanded, older messages start collapsed
  • Open in Gmail → link at the bottom of each thread opens the thread directly in Gmail
⚠ Gmail must be connected in Setup for emails to appear. The extension uses Chrome's identity API (chrome.identity.getAuthToken) — no password is stored.
Meetings

The Meetings tab shows notes from Granola, an AI meeting notes tool. Notes are fetched by searching Granola's MCP API for the company name.

How meeting notes are fetched

1
Search by company name + contacts
The background worker calls the Granola MCP list_meetings tool with the company name and, when available, known contact first names as search signals. The search window spans the last 90 days.
2
Match by title or attendee
A meeting matches if its title contains the company name or a known contact name. First-name-only matching (≥3 chars) is supported for verified company contacts so "Noah" matches "Noah Maffitt".
3
Full transcripts fetched
For each matched meeting, get_meeting is called to retrieve the full transcript. Structured per-meeting data (title, date, attendees, transcript) is cached on the entry as cachedMeetings.
4
Auto-loaded into AI Chat context
Transcripts are automatically injected into the AI Chat panel when it opens — no manual "Load meeting notes" click required. Each meeting is shown in the AI system prompt with its relative date (e.g., "3 days ago").
💡 Granola uses OAuth 2.0 with PKCE (S256 challenge). Connect it in Setup. The Meetings tab shows formatted notes; the AI Chat panel uses the raw transcripts for richer context.
Known Contacts

Known Contacts are people from a company's domain who have verifiably engaged with you — via email or calendar — as distinct from API-guessed leadership.

Detection — Email

When the Emails tab loads, every From: and To: header is scanned. Any email address whose domain matches the company's website domain (e.g. *@productgenius.ai) is saved as a Known Contact with:

  • name — parsed from the display name in the email header, or derived from the email local part
  • email — the full email address
  • source — "email" (future: "calendar")
  • detectedAt — Unix timestamp

Detection — Calendar

Calendar-based detection is planned. When implemented, attendees from Google Calendar events with company-domain email addresses will be added automatically.

Leadership email matching

When a Known Contact's name fuzzy-matches a leader in the Leadership panel (same first name + last name, case-insensitive), that leader's card shows their email address below their title. This happens automatically when new contacts are detected.

Where contacts appear

  • company.html — Known Contacts panel (top of right column) — all detected contacts with teal avatar, name, email, source badge
  • company.html — Leadership panel — matched email shown below the leader's role
  • Sidepanel — Known Contacts section — appears above Leadership when contacts exist for the current company
ℹ Contacts persist on the entry in savedCompanies. They are detected once and remain even if you don't open the Emails tab again.
AI Chat

Every company and opportunity page has a contextual AI chat panel powered by Claude. The chat is pre-loaded with everything CompanyIntel knows about that company so you can ask freeform questions without copy-pasting context.

What's included in the AI context

  • Today's date — absolute date + timestamp so the AI can reason about timelines
  • Company profile — name, status, tags, intelligence summary, funding, employees
  • Leadership — founders and executives with titles
  • Known Contacts — verified people with email and source
  • Job details — role, match score, job description, salary, perks
  • Employee reviews — Glassdoor / Blind / Reddit snippets
  • Email history — up to 20 emails with subject, sender, date, and body snippet
  • Meeting transcripts — all matched Granola meetings, each labeled with relative time (e.g., "3 days ago"), up to 4,000 chars per meeting

Chat behavior

  • Session-only history — chat history is fresh each time you open a company page, keeping context clean and focused on the current company
  • Follow-up chips — after each AI reply, "Say more" and "Key takeaways" chips appear for quick follow-ups
  • Action buttons — "Load emails" and "Load meeting notes" manually refresh those context sources mid-conversation
  • Auto context — emails and meeting notes are injected automatically if already cached; Granola is fetched in the background when the panel opens
  • Minimal mode — the floating chat widget (bottom-right of company.html) uses a compact version with only the Clear button
💡 The AI context is rebuilt on every message send, so if Granola finishes loading after you opened the panel, the next message you send will include the full transcripts.
Pipeline Celebrations

When you drag an opportunity card into certain pipeline stages, a confetti animation fires and an optional sound plays. The type of confetti and the sound are fully configurable per stage.

Default celebrations

  • Applied — 👍 thumbs-up emojis, pop sound
  • Conversations in Progress / Mutual Interest — classic confetti burst, pop sound
  • Offer Stage / Accepted / Referral — 🤑 money emojis + 💵 dollar signs, cha-ching sound
  • Stalled — 🛑 stop signs, no sound
  • Rejected / DQ'd / Closed Lost — ✌️ peace signs, farewell voice (rotates: "peace", "adios", "see ya", "bye")

Customizing celebrations

1
Open the Stage Editor
In the Opportunity pipeline, click the gear icon (⚙) next to the pipeline header to open the Stage Editor.
2
Switch to the Celebrations tab
Click the 🎉 Celebrations tab in the editor. Each stage is listed with its current confetti type and sound choice.
3
Set confetti type and sound per stage
Use the dropdowns to choose from: None, Classic confetti, 👍 Thumbs up, 🤑 Money, 🛑 Stop signs, ✌️ Peace signs — and sounds: None, 🎉 Pop, 💰 Cha-ching, 👋 Farewell voice. Stages set to "None" fire no animation.
4
Save
Click Save to persist. Your choices are saved in chrome.storage.local as stageCelebrations. Use Reset to defaults to restore the original behavior.
ℹ Celebrations only apply to the Opportunity pipeline. The Company pipeline has no celebrations tab (stage moves there don't trigger animations).
Integrations

CompanyIntel connects several APIs to power research, AI analysis, and activity tracking. All keys are stored locally in chrome.storage.sync and never sent to any server other than the respective API.

📧
Gmail
Used to fetch email threads related to companies. Uses Chrome's built-in OAuth via chrome.identity.getAuthToken. No password stored. Requires the Gmail read scope.
Connect in Setup
🌿
Granola
AI meeting notes tool. Connected via OAuth 2.0 with PKCE. Meeting notes are searched by company name via the Granola MCP API.
Connect in Setup
🔵
Apollo.io
B2B data provider. Used for firmographics: employee count, funding, founded year, industry, website, LinkedIn URL. Requires an Apollo API key.
Add API key
🤖
Anthropic / Claude
Powers company intelligence summaries, job match scoring, and the Ask AI chat panel. Uses Claude Sonnet 4.5 by default. Requires an Anthropic API key.

🔍
Serper
Google Search API. Used to find leadership profiles, reviews, hiring signals, and company product info. Requires a Serper API key.

Data & Storage

All data is stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to any CompanyIntel server.

Storage keys

  • savedCompanies — array of all company/opportunity entries (in chrome.storage.local)
  • researchCache — cached research results keyed by company name, used to avoid re-running API calls (in chrome.storage.local)
  • opportunityStages / companyStages — custom pipeline stage configurations
  • stageCelebrations — per-stage confetti type and sound overrides (in chrome.storage.local)
  • prefs — your profile and job preferences, used for Job Match scoring (in chrome.storage.sync, synced across devices)
  • gmailConnected — boolean flag for Gmail connection state
  • granolaToken — Granola OAuth access token
  • ci_co_layout_[id] — per-company panel layout saved in localStorage
  • ci_co_collapsed_[id] — per-company panel collapse state saved in localStorage
💡 Research cache entries do not expire automatically. To re-run research for a company, click the Refresh button in the sidepanel when on that company's page.
