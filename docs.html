Overview

Coop.ai turns your browser into a personal CRM for job searching. Research companies on demand, track opportunities through a pipeline, and get AI-powered advice with your full search context already loaded.

i Nothing fires without a click. Research, scoring, and enrichment only run when you take action. No API credits are spent on page load or panel open.
Company Research

Visit any company page, click Research, and get firmographics, leadership, reviews, and a synthesized profile. Cached for 24 hours.

Opportunity Pipeline

Track companies and jobs through a Kanban board or sortable table. Drag cards between stages, swipe to triage, filter by score or status.

Emails, Meetings & Contacts

Gmail threads and Granola meeting transcripts attach to the right company automatically. People you've communicated with surface as Known Contacts.

Coop (AI Advisor)

Chat with an AI that already knows your profile, the company, the job, your emails, meetings, and past conversations. Available on every page.

Job Scoring

Every saved job gets a 1-10 score across five dimensions. Scores are math, not guesswork -- you can verify every number.

Apply Queue New

Swipe through scored opportunities one at a time. Pass or advance, then open the application with Coop already bound to that job.

Coop Memory

Coop remembers what you tell him across sessions. Stories, preferences, and corrections persist automatically. Manage in Coop settings.

Model Fallback

If one AI model hits a rate limit, Coop automatically tries the next. Click the model badge to switch manually.

Sidepanel & Coop

The sidepanel detects what company or job you're browsing, shows saved data instantly, and gives you Coop chat with full context. Nothing fires until you click.

First use

1
Browse to any company page
Open a company website, LinkedIn page, or job posting. The sidepanel detects the company from the URL and page content.
2
Click Research
Coop pulls firmographics, leadership, reviews, and hiring signals, then synthesizes a company profile. Results cache for 24 hours.
3
Save to pipeline
Save Company adds a company record. Send to Coop on a job posting saves the opportunity and queues it for scoring.

Coop binding

Coop auto-binds to whatever company or opportunity is on the current tab. His chat loads with emails, meetings, contacts, and job details for that entry. To focus Coop on a different entry, click the paperclip in the chat header and pick any saved entry.

Sidepanel sections

  • Company Overview -- employees, funding, industry, founded year
  • Hiring Signals -- open roles from LinkedIn Jobs, Greenhouse, and Lever
  • Known Contacts -- people from the company found in your Gmail or calendar
  • Leadership -- executives sourced from LinkedIn and news
  • Reviews & Signal -- Glassdoor, Blind, RepVue, and Reddit snippets

Home tab: Tasks

When no company is detected, the sidepanel shows a task list pulled from email extraction across your pipeline. Filter by priority (High / Normal / Low) and date (Overdue / Today / Upcoming).

i View in CRM appears after saving a company. Click it to open the full company detail page.

Opportunity Pipeline

Any saved company can become an opportunity. Opportunities get their own Kanban board and table view, separate from general company research.

Default stages

AI Scoring Queue I Want to Apply Applied Intro Requested Conversations in Progress Offer Stage Accepted Rejected / DQ'd

Stages are fully customizable. Click the gear icon next to the pipeline header to edit.

Kanban view

Drag cards between stage columns to advance or reject them. Each card shows the score, action status (your court / their court), and next step. Click any card to expand it with score details and swipe controls.

Table view

  • Sort by any column -- click a header to toggle ascending/descending
  • Column picker -- click the grid button to toggle 16 available columns on or off
  • Inline editing -- change stage and star rating directly in the table row

Stat cards

The metric cards at the top of the dashboard (counts by stage, action status, etc.) are clickable. Click any card to see the specific opportunities behind the number.

Swipe gestures

Click any Kanban card to open the compact view. Swipe right to advance to the next stage, swipe left to reject. Labels update to show the actual stage names.

Action On + Coop next steps

When a stage changes, Coop auto-sets "Action On" (your court vs. their court) and writes a suggested next step, prefixed with Coop → . Your manual next steps are never overwritten.

Apply Queue
New

A swipe-to-triage surface for reviewing scored opportunities one at a time. See the score breakdown, qualifications, and flags, then decide: pass or advance.

  • Full score card -- 5-dimension breakdown, qualification list, fired flags with evidence, and Coop's verdict
  • Pass sends to Rejected/DQ. Interested moves forward in the pipeline.
  • Open Application opens the job URL and auto-binds Coop to that opportunity in the sidepanel
  • Add to experience -- on any unmet qualification, jump to My Profile to fill the gap, then re-score

Active Review mode

Same UI, but for re-evaluating opportunities at Applied or later stages. Open via queue.html?mode=dq. Buttons relabel to DQ and Still Active.

Send to Coop

The save flow for jobs. One click extracts the job description, saves the entry, and queues it for scoring.

Three actions

  • Send to Coop -- extracts job data from the page, saves, and queues for scoring. Available as an on-page pill button (LinkedIn, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, Ashby, Rippling, Workable, YC, generic career pages) and in the sidepanel.
  • Send to Coop + Research -- same as above, plus runs the full enrichment pipeline (firmographics, leadership, product deep-dive, competitive landscape). Sidepanel only.
  • Save Company -- for non-job pages. Saves a company record with no scoring.

What happens when you click

Duplicate handling

Saving the same role twice enriches the existing entry with new data (longer JD, updated firmographics, salary info) and re-queues for scoring. No duplicates are created.

What gets saved

  • Company name, LinkedIn URL, website
  • Job title, job URL, full description text
  • Salary, OTE, equity (auto-extracted from posting)
  • Work arrangement, employment type, location, perks
  • On LinkedIn: employee count, industry, HQ, founded year, seniority, skills, applicant count, recruiter, connections count -- see LinkedIn Data Capture
i You don't need to click "see more" on LinkedIn job descriptions. The content script expands them automatically before saving.

How Scoring Works

Coop scores jobs across five dimensions. The AI identifies which of your configured flags fire and extracts qualifications; all actual scoring math runs in deterministic JavaScript. You can verify every number.

i Cost: ~$0.005-0.015 per score (Haiku by default). Configure the scoring model in Coop settings.

Scoring triggers

  • On save -- automatic, every time you save a job
  • Manual re-score -- click "Refresh analysis" on any opportunity
  • Optional auto-rescore -- profile changes, salary changes, or new interaction data can trigger rescores. All off by default; enable in Coop settings.

Hard disqualification

A Hard DQ only fires when a severity-5 dealbreaker matches with evidence. This caps the overall score at 4. If the AI suggests a DQ but no sev-5 flag actually fired, the DQ is suppressed.

ICP → Flags Auto-sync

Coop auto-generates scoring flags from your Role ICP, Company ICP, and Work arrangement settings in My Profile. Every time you save those sections, the flags refresh automatically.

What gets generated

  • Work arrangement -- check every arrangement you're willing to accept. Coop generates green flags for checked options and red flags for ones you won't do. Example: Remote only checked = severity-5 hard DQ flag for "in-office or hybrid required".
  • Role ICP -- target function, seniority, scope, and selling motion become green flags in the Role Fit dimension
  • Company ICP -- stage, size, industry, and culture markers become green flags in Company Fit and Culture Fit dimensions

ICP-generated flags show a gear icon label. You can edit them freely -- once edited, they won't be overwritten by future ICP syncs. Delete one and it regenerates on the next save.

Score Breakdown

Every score shows a visual breakdown of five weighted dimensions. Each is scored 1-10 and displayed as a colored bar: green (7+), amber (4-6), red (1-3).

  • Qualification Fit (20%) -- how well your skills and experience match the job requirements
  • Role Fit (20%) -- type of role, seniority, and scope alignment with your target
  • Culture Fit (25%) -- leadership style, remote culture, growth signals from Glassdoor/RepVue/Reddit reviews. Emails and meetings weigh heavily when available.
  • Company Fit (20%) -- stage, industry, and product-market fit based on firmographics
  • Comp Fit (15%) -- base and OTE compared against your floor and strong-offer thresholds

The formula: Q*20 + R*20 + Cu*25 + Co*20 + Comp*15 = X/100 → 1-10. The math row at the bottom of every queue card shows the exact calculation.

Flag curation

Click the X on any red or green flag to dismiss it. Dismissed flags are excluded from future rescores. Flags use gradient colors -- darker means more important.

Qualification Matching

Coop extracts every requirement from a job posting and matches each one against your resume, experience entries, skill tags, Story Time, and ICP settings.

How to read the results

  • ✓ Met -- clear evidence in your profile. Full credit.
  • ~ Partial -- related experience but with a gap. Half credit.
  • ✗ Unmet -- not found in your background. No credit.
  • ✗ Not in profile (italic label) -- no evidence found in any profile source. Treated as unmet. Fix it by updating Experience or Skills in My Profile, then re-score.

Score formula

Computed in JavaScript, not by the AI: (met * 1 + partial * 0.5) / total * 10. Comp-related qualifications are excluded from this count since comp has its own dimension.

Fixing gaps

Click Add to experience on any unmet item to jump to the relevant My Profile section. After updating, re-score to see the improvement. You can also dismiss irrelevant qualifications with the X button -- dismissed items persist across sessions.

Cost Tracking

A persistent cost pill in the pipeline header shows your running spend. Click it for a full breakdown by provider, by model, and recent calls.

  • By Provider -- Claude, OpenAI, Apollo, Serper, Granola with request counts and costs
  • By Model -- sorted by cost, shows which model is driving spend
  • Recent Calls -- last 20 API calls with model, tokens, and per-call cost

All tracking is local. Call log entries expire after 24 hours. View detailed per-entry costs in the company header. Configure cost-related settings in Coop settings.

Emails & Tasks

The Emails tab in the company view shows Gmail threads related to the company. Each email fetch also extracts action items so tasks surface automatically.

How it works

1
Click the Emails tab
Emails load on first click. Gmail is searched by company domain, name, and LinkedIn slug.
2
Threads grouped, contacts extracted
Messages group by thread. Anyone whose email domain matches the company is saved as a Known Contact.
3
Tasks extracted
Action items are pulled from new messages automatically. Tasks show a "from email" source label. Hit Re-scan to re-extract from cached emails.

Coop Assist (writing helper) New

Coop Assist is an ambient writing helper on every page. Type ~20 characters in any text field (LinkedIn DMs, Gmail compose, application forms) and a small pill appears.

  • Local heuristics (free) -- flags cliches, LLM-tells, exclamation overuse, generic sign-offs
  • AI rewrites -- pick a mode: In my voice, Tighten, Punchier, or Warmer
  • Spell & grammar -- after 1.5s of inactivity, Coop proofs for spelling, grammar, and punctuation. One-click fixes.
  • Pause 1h silences Coop everywhere. Add custom blocked domains in My Profile > Coop Assist.
! Gmail must be connected in Integrations for emails to appear. The extension uses Chrome's identity API -- no password is stored.
Meetings

The Meetings tab shows notes from Granola, matched by company name and known contact names. Transcripts load into Coop's context automatically.

How matching works

1
Search by company + contacts
Granola is searched for the company name and known contact first names. Covers the last 90 days.
2
Match by title or attendee
A meeting matches if its title contains the company name or a known contact name.
3
Transcripts auto-load into Coop
Full transcripts are injected into Coop's context. No manual "load meeting notes" step needed.
i Connect Granola in Integrations. The Meetings tab shows formatted notes; Coop uses the raw transcripts for richer context.
Known Contacts

People from a company's domain who have communicated with you via email or calendar. Distinct from API-sourced leadership.

How contacts are detected

  • Email -- any sender or recipient whose domain matches the company's website domain
  • Calendar -- Google Calendar attendees whose email domain matches
  • Each contact records why it was linked (domain match, thread subject, event title) so you can verify attribution

Leadership email matching

When a Known Contact's name matches a leader in the Leadership panel, that leader's card shows their email address. Happens automatically.

! Domain matching can be loose for common domains. Check the source attribution on any contact that looks wrong and remove it if needed.
Coop (AI Chat)

Coop is available on every surface: sidepanel, company view, opportunity view, pipeline dashboard (as Pipeline Advisor), and the Apply Queue. He's pre-loaded with your profile, the bound entry's context, and memories from past sessions.

What to ask Coop

Application help

"Why am I interested in this role?" -- "Help me answer this free-form question" -- "Draft a cover letter"

Interview prep

"What should I ask in my next call?" -- "Summarize my meeting with Sarah" -- "What did they say about comp?"

Research questions

"What's the culture like?" -- "How does this role compare to the Acme opportunity?" -- "Red flags?"

Pipeline strategy

"Which opportunities should I prioritize?" -- "What's stalled?" -- "Draft a follow-up email"

Model picker

Click the model badge in the chat header to cycle between available models. The fallback chain (GPT-4.1 mini > Haiku > Sonnet > GPT-4.1) kicks in automatically on rate limits. A note shows which model actually responded.

Chat behavior

  • Session-only history -- fresh context every page visit. Coop Memory is the long-term store.
  • Auto-bind -- Coop binds to the detected entry on the current tab
  • Manual bind -- paperclip button lets you pin Coop to any saved entry
  • Pipeline Advisor -- global chat on the dashboard with whole-pipeline context
Coop Memory

Coop remembers things you tell him across sessions. Stories, preferences, corrections, and project context persist automatically and surface in future conversations.

What it remembers

  • About you -- background, preferences, constraints
  • Feedback -- how you want Coop to behave (corrections, tone adjustments)
  • Projects -- specific things you're working on
  • References -- stories and anecdotes (often from the gap-to-story loop where Coop asks about experience you have that wasn't in your profile)

Where to manage it

Coop settings > Memory shows all entries. Delete anything that's wrong or stale. The memory model is configurable separately from the chat model.

Knowledge documents

Your profile, preferences, and conversation learnings are auto-compiled into granular knowledge documents that Coop can selectively access via tools. Instead of loading your entire profile every time, Coop requests only the sections relevant to the question (e.g. just Compensation and Role ICP for a salary question).

  • When they refresh -- on extension startup, and reactively whenever you edit your profile/preferences or Coop learns something new from a conversation (2-second debounce)
  • Cost -- compilation costs nothing (pure local text parsing, no API calls)
  • What's extracted -- Story, Experience, Skills, Operating Principles, Voice, FAQ, Resume, Education, Links, Role ICP, Company ICP, Green/Red Flags, Compensation, Location, and accumulated Learnings
  • Transparency -- after each chat response, a collapsible panel shows exactly which sections Coop loaded

View all compiled documents and their token counts in Coop settings > Memory > Knowledge documents.

Operating Principles

Rules that control how Coop interprets dealbreakers, calibrates scores, and shapes draft tone. Edit in Coop settings > Personality.

What to put in them

  • How to interpret your dealbreakers ("remote means fully remote")
  • How to calibrate scores ("7+ is reserved for genuine fits")
  • Tone rules for drafts (cover letters, intro requests)
  • How to weigh title vs scope vs comp when they conflict
i Operating Principles feed into both chat and scoring. Editing them changes how Coop scores future opportunities -- consider re-scoring open roles after a big change.

Pipeline Celebrations

Dragging an opportunity into certain stages fires a confetti animation and optional sound. Customize per stage via the gear icon > Celebrations tab in the pipeline header.

Data & Storage

All data stays in your browser. Nothing is sent to any Coop.ai server.

Where data lives

  • savedCompanies -- all company and opportunity entries (chrome.storage.local)
  • researchCache -- research results, 24h TTL (chrome.storage.local)
  • coopMemory -- Coop's long-term memory entries (chrome.storage.local)
  • prefs -- your profile and job preferences (chrome.storage.sync, syncs across devices)
  • coopConfig -- model assignments, auto-rescore settings, scoring preferences (chrome.storage.local)
  • API keys -- stored locally via Integrations page (not synced)
  • Panel layouts and table columns -- per-company in localStorage
i Research cache expires after 24 hours. Click Refresh in the sidepanel to force a re-run before that.
