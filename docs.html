Overview

Coop.ai is a Chrome extension that turns your browser into a personal CRM for your job search. It researches companies on demand, tracks every opportunity through a Kanban pipeline, ties in your Gmail and Granola meeting notes, and gives you Coop — an AI advisor with the full context of your search already loaded.

i No auto-fire on page load. Research, scoring, and enrichment only run when you click a button. The sidepanel will detect a company and offer to research it, but it will not burn API credits without an explicit action.
On-Demand Company Research

Visit any company website or LinkedIn page, hit Research, and Coop pulls firmographics (Apollo), leadership and reviews (Serper), and synthesizes a profile (Claude). Cached 24h.

Unified Pipeline

Companies and opportunities live in a single savedCompanies[] store. Tag any company as an opportunity to track it through your customizable Kanban or Table pipeline view. Tasks (action items from emails) live in the header nav alongside the pipeline.

Activity Auto-Wire

Gmail threads (by domain), Granola meeting transcripts, and calendar contacts attach themselves to the right entry. Email threads can also generate tasks via Haiku.

Coop (AI Advisor)

Contextual chat with your full profile, the company, the job, emails, meetings, and your Operating Principles all pre-loaded. Auto-binds to the detected entry; pin a different one with the paperclip button.

Apply Queue + Active Review

Tinder-style swipe triage for unscored opportunities, plus an Active Review mode for re-evaluating Applied+ stages. Open Application auto-binds Coop to that opportunity.

Operating Principles

A user-editable textarea in Career OS that controls how Coop interprets dealbreakers, scores roles, and writes drafts. Replaces the old hardcoded opinions in code.

Coop Memory

Coop passively extracts typed insights (about you, feedback, projects, references) from chat, with a 60s debounce. Future conversations surface relevant memories automatically.

Model Fallback Chain

Chat cycles GPT-4.1 mini → Haiku → Sonnet → GPT-4.1 on rate limits or errors. The UI shows a fallback note. Click the model badge to manually cycle.

Sidepanel & Coop

The sidepanel is your research-on-demand surface and your home for Coop chat. It detects what company or job posting you're on, surfaces any saved data instantly from cache, and waits for you to click before spending API credits.

🔍
Sidepanel detecting a company and showing cached research with Coop chat below

How research works

1
Company detected
The content script reads the page URL and DOM to identify the company. Detectors exist for LinkedIn, Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, Ashby, and a generic fallback (og:site_name, page title, domain).
2
Cache check first
If the company is already saved or has cached research, the sidepanel loads that data instantly. No API calls fire on detection or sidepanel open.
3
You click Research
Apollo returns firmographics. Serper runs parallel searches for leadership, reviews, jobs, and product info. Claude Haiku synthesizes everything into structured JSON. Results cache for 24 hours in researchCache.
4
Save to pipeline
Click Save Company to add to your CRM. On a job posting, Save as Opportunity captures the role and runs Job Match scoring against your preferences.

Coop bind: auto and manual

When the sidepanel detects a saved company or opportunity on the current tab, Coop auto-binds to that entry — meaning chat is loaded with the full context for it (emails, meetings, contacts, job details, memory). The bound entry is shown in the chat header.

If you want Coop to focus on a different entry (e.g. you're filling out an unrelated application but want context on a specific company), click the paperclip button in the chat header and pick any saved entry. The pin persists until you change it or navigate away.

Sidepanel sections

  • Company Overview — employees, funding, industry, founded year, one-liner, category
  • Hiring Signals — open roles found on LinkedIn Jobs, Greenhouse, and Lever
  • Known Contacts — people from the company's domain found in your Gmail (appears once contacts are detected)
  • Leadership — founders and executives sourced from LinkedIn and news results
  • Reviews & Signal — Glassdoor, Blind, RepVue, and Reddit snippets
💡 The View in CRM link appears after you save a company. It opens the full-screen company view for that entry.

Opportunity Pipeline

Any saved company can be tagged as an Opportunity. Opportunities appear in their own Kanban pipeline in the Saved view, tracked separately from your general company research.

📋
Kanban pipeline with opportunity cards showing scores, action status, and stage columns

Architecture

Opportunities are not separate records — they are companies with isOpportunity: true set. This means a company record and its opportunity share a single entry, eliminating the sync issues that come with duplicated data.

Opportunity-specific fields are stored directly on the company entry:

  • isOpportunity — boolean flag
  • jobStage — current pipeline stage (separate from status which tracks company research stage)
  • jobTitle — role title
  • jobUrl — link to the original job posting
  • jobMatch — AI match score and analysis
  • jobSnapshot — salary, work arrangement, employment type

Default pipeline stages

AI Scoring Queue I Want to Apply Applied Intro Requested Conversations in Progress Offer Stage Accepted Rejected / DQ'd

Stages are fully customizable in Setup. Custom stages persist in chrome.storage.local as opportunityStages.

Job Match scoring

When you save a job posting from the sidepanel, Claude AI scores the role (1–10) against your background, preferred roles, work arrangement, and salary floor set in Career OS. The score and a breakdown (strong fits, red flags, verdict) are saved on the entry and shown on Kanban cards.

Job snapshot — Perks vs Salary

When a job posting is saved, LinkedIn chips are scanned and classified: amounts matching salary patterns ($120K, $120,000) go to Base Salary; chip text containing words like stipend, allowance, reimbursement, or subsidy is classified as a Perk instead. Perks appear as teal chips below the salary row so items like "$50/mo internet stipend" are never confused with base compensation.

Stat card drill-down

The metric cards on the saved dashboard (counts by stage, action-on, etc.) are clickable. Click any card to open a drill-down modal listing the specific opportunities behind the number, with quick links into each entry.

Table view

Switch to the ☰ Table view from the header to see all opportunities in a sortable database table.

  • Sort by any column — click any column header to sort ascending or descending. Click again to toggle direction. An arrow indicator shows the active sort.
  • Column picker (⊞) — click the ⊞ button in the table header to open a column selector. Toggle any of 16 available columns on or off. Active columns persist per session via localStorage.
  • Available columns — Company, Stage, Score, Stars, Action On, Next Step, Next Step Date, Industry, Employees, Funding, Applied Date, Last Activity, Tags, Saved Date, Role, Job URL.
  • Inline stage editing — change the pipeline stage directly from a dropdown in the table row without opening the full entry.
  • Inline star rating — click any star in the row to update the rating in place.

Tasks (header nav)

The ✓ Tasks link in the top header bar opens your extracted email tasks across all pipeline companies. Tasks is a top-level destination, not a view variant — it replaces the pipeline content area while active, and the "Opportunity Pipeline" heading is hidden when Tasks is open.

Swipe gesture — stage-aware labels

When you click any Kanban card to open it in the compact tinder-style view and swipe it left or right, the labels and behavior are now context-aware for every stage — not just the Apply Queue:

  • Swipe right — advances to the next stage (green label shows the actual next stage name). Updates the entry in storage immediately.
  • Swipe left — moves to the rejected/DQ'd stage (red label shows the actual rejected stage name).
  • In the AI Scoring Queue specifically: swipe right = "Interested", left = "Pass" (as before).
  • In Apply Queue mode: swipe right = "Apply", left = "Pass" (as before).

Action On auto-set + Coop next steps

When Coop automatically changes the Action On field due to a stage transition, it also writes a Next Step to describe what should happen next. These auto-generated next steps are prefixed with Coop → so you always know the source.

  • Auto next steps only write if the Next Step field is blank, or if it was previously written by Coop (starts with Coop → ). Manual next steps are never overwritten.
  • The nextStepSource: 'coop-auto' field is stored on the entry to distinguish Coop-generated steps from user-entered ones.
  • Stage-to-step examples: Applied → "Coop → Follow up if no response in 5-7 days", Offer Stage → "Coop → Review offer details and respond within 48-72 hours".
Apply Queue & Active Review
New

The Apply Queue is a Tinder-style swipe surface for quickly triaging unscored opportunities. The same page in DQ mode (queue.html?mode=dq) becomes Active Review, which lets you re-evaluate opportunities already at Applied or beyond.

🃏
Apply Queue card with score breakdown, qualification lines, and swipe buttons

Apply Queue (default)

  • One opportunity at a time — full card with score, breakdown, qualifications, and Coop's verdict
  • Pass sends to Closed/DQ; Interested moves it forward in the pipeline
  • Open Application opens the original job URL in a new tab and auto-binds Coop in the sidepanel to that opportunity, so when you start filling out the form Coop already has the full context loaded

Emails & Tasks

The Emails tab in the company view shows Gmail threads related to the company, lazy-loaded the first time you click the tab. Each fetch piggybacks task extraction so action items surface automatically.

📧
Email threads grouped by company with task extraction badges

How emails are fetched

1
Query built
A Gmail search query is constructed using the company domain (from:*@domain.com OR to:*@domain.com), company name, and LinkedIn slug. Up to 20 messages are fetched.
2
Full message bodies fetched
Each message is fetched with format=full to get the complete body. MIME parts are parsed recursively to find text/plain content.
3
Quoted content stripped
Reply chains, >-quoted lines, and On [date]... wrote: headers are stripped so each message shows only its new content.
4
Threads grouped, contacts extracted
Messages are grouped by threadId. Any sender/recipient whose email domain matches the company domain is saved as a Known Contact on the entry.

Thread UI

  • Each thread shows as a collapsible card: subject, participants, date, message count
  • Click a thread header to expand/collapse all messages in that thread
  • Within a thread, each individual message can be expanded by clicking its sender row — the latest message starts expanded, older messages start collapsed
  • Open in Gmail → link at the bottom of each thread opens the thread directly in Gmail
  • Delete — per-email delete from the company inbox view removes it from the cached thread (Gmail is untouched)

Coop Assist (writing helper) New

Coop Assist is an ambient writing helper that runs on every page. Focus into any text field — LinkedIn DMs, Gmail compose, application forms, anywhere — and once you have ~20 characters typed, a small ✨ Coop pill appears near the bottom of the field.

  • Phase 1 — Local heuristics (free, instant): flags clichés ("I hope this email finds you well", "circle back"), LLM-tells ("delve into", "tapestry"), exclamation overuse, generic sign-offs ("Best regards" → suggests "—Matt"), and double-spaces.
  • Phase 2 — AI rewrites: click the pill to open the panel and pick a mode — In my voice, Tighten, Punchier, or Warmer. Coop sends the text to Claude Haiku with your voice profile and returns a rewrite. Preview, then Apply or Discard.
  • Phase 3 — Spell & grammar (Grammarly-style): 1.5 seconds after you stop typing, Coop sends the text to Claude Haiku for proofreading. Spelling errors, grammar mistakes, and punctuation problems come back as one-click fixes in the panel — each shows the original and the proposed correction. Apply individually or pick a whole-text rewrite. Cached by exact text so re-opens are free.
  • Apply uses a React-safe native setter so it works inside controlled inputs (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday, Gmail).
  • Pause 1h button silences Coop everywhere from inside the panel or from preferences.
  • Blocklist: banking, healthcare, gov, and login pages are always excluded. Add custom domains in Preferences → Coop Assist.
  • Privacy: Phase 1 makes zero network calls. Phase 2 only sends text when you explicitly click a rewrite button.

Task extraction New

Every email fetch piggybacks a Haiku call that extracts action items from the new messages. Tasks appear on the opportunity card and in the right sidebar with a "from email" source pill so you know where they came from. Hit Re-scan to re-extract from the current cached emails without re-fetching Gmail.

⚠ Gmail must be connected in Setup for emails to appear. The extension uses Chrome's identity API (chrome.identity.getAuthToken) — no password is stored.
Meetings

The Meetings tab shows notes from Granola, an AI meeting notes tool. Notes are fetched by searching Granola's MCP API for the company name.

🗓️
Meetings tab with Granola transcripts matched by company name and contacts

How meeting notes are fetched

1
Search by company name + contacts
The background worker calls the Granola MCP list_meetings tool with the company name and, when available, known contact first names as search signals. The search window spans the last 90 days.
2
Match by title or attendee
A meeting matches if its title contains the company name or a known contact name. First-name-only matching (≥3 chars) is supported for verified company contacts so "Noah" matches "Noah Maffitt".
3
Full transcripts fetched
For each matched meeting, get_meeting is called to retrieve the full transcript. Structured per-meeting data (title, date, attendees, transcript) is cached on the entry as cachedMeetings.
4
Auto-loaded into AI Chat context
Transcripts are automatically injected into the AI Chat panel when it opens — no manual "Load meeting notes" click required. Each meeting is shown in the AI system prompt with its relative date (e.g., "3 days ago").
💡 Granola uses OAuth 2.0 with PKCE (S256 challenge). Connect it in Setup. The Meetings tab shows formatted notes; the AI Chat panel uses the raw transcripts for richer context.
Known Contacts

Known Contacts are people from a company's domain who have verifiably engaged with you — via email or calendar — as distinct from API-guessed leadership.

Detection — Email

When the Emails tab loads, every From: and To: header is scanned. Any email address whose domain matches the company's website domain (e.g. *@productgenius.ai) is saved as a Known Contact with:

  • name — parsed from the display name in the email header, or derived from the email local part
  • email — the full email address
  • source — "email" (future: "calendar")
  • detectedAt — Unix timestamp

Detection — Calendar

Google Calendar attendees whose email domain matches the company's website domain are added the same way as email contacts, with source: "calendar".

Association traceability

Each contact records why it got linked to the entry — domain match, email thread subject, calendar event title, etc. The Known Contacts panel surfaces this source so you can sanity-check whether a contact was correctly attributed (and remove it if not).

Leadership email matching

When a Known Contact's name fuzzy-matches a leader in the Leadership panel (same first name + last name, case-insensitive), that leader's card shows their email address below their title. This happens automatically when new contacts are detected.

Where contacts appear

  • company.html — Known Contacts panel (top of right column) — all detected contacts with teal avatar, name, email, source badge
  • company.html — Leadership panel — matched email shown below the leader's role
  • Sidepanel — Known Contacts section — appears above Leadership when contacts exist for the current company
ℹ Contacts persist on the entry in savedCompanies. They are detected once and remain even if you don't open the Emails tab again.
Coop (AI Chat)

Coop is the AI advisor baked into every surface of the extension — the sidepanel, company view, opportunity view, the saved dashboard (as Pipeline Advisor), and the Apply Queue. He's pre-loaded with your profile, your operating principles, the bound entry's full context, and any relevant memories from past sessions.

💬
Coop chat panel bound to an opportunity with model badge and context header

What's in the system prompt

  • Today's date — absolute date + timestamp for timeline reasoning
  • Your profile — Career OS resume, Story Time, salary floor, work arrangement, etc.
  • Operating Principles — your editable interpretation rules from Career OS
  • Coop Memory — relevant typed insights from past chats (user / feedback / project / reference)
  • Company profile — name, status, tags, intelligence, funding, employees, reviews
  • Leadership and Known Contacts — with email + source where available
  • Job details — role, score, breakdown, qualifications, JD text, salary, perks
  • Email history — up to 20 cached threads with stripped bodies
  • Meeting transcripts — all matched Granola meetings, tagged with relative time, with a per-meeting char budget

Tier routing

The context builder picks a tier based on whether Coop is bound to an entry. Bound chats get the full-context tier (everything above). Unbound or global chats use a lighter tier. Vision is gated separately (see Screen Share).

Model fallback chain

Coop calls go through chatWithFallback(), which tries your selected model first and then cycles through whatever else has API keys configured. The chain is:

GPT-4.1 mini → Claude Haiku → Claude Sonnet → GPT-4.1

Providers without keys are skipped. If a fallback answers, the chat shows a small note telling you which model actually responded. You can manually cycle the active model by clicking the model badge in the chat header.

Three model picks (not one)

  • Chat model — what Coop uses to answer messages
  • Coop autofill model — what scores opportunities, drafts, and extracts qualifications
  • Coop memory model — what synthesizes passive insights into typed memory entries

All three are configurable independently in Career OS > Coop settings.

Gap → story memory loop

When Coop flags an experience gap during scoring or chat, he asks for a story. If you tell him about relevant experience you have, he confirms it and writes that story into Coop Memory as a reference insight. Future scoring runs and chats see it.

Chat behavior

  • Session-only history — nothing persists between sessions; memory is the long-term store
  • Follow-up chips — quick "Say more" / "Key takeaways" after each reply
  • Auto-bind — sidepanel Coop binds to the detected entry automatically
  • Manual bind — the paperclip button lets you pin Coop to any saved entry
  • Pipeline Advisor — the global chat on the saved dashboard with whole-pipeline context, no specific entry binding
i The Context Window for the bound entry auto-refreshes once per day on page load, so Coop's view of an entry doesn't go stale across days without you needing to manually re-research.
Coop Memory

Coop Memory is the long-term store of things Coop has learned about you across chats. It replaces the old "type the same context every conversation" pattern.

How it gets populated

A passive insight extractor runs after each chat reply with a ~60 second debounce. It reads the recent exchange, decides whether anything new is worth remembering, and writes typed entries into coopMemory. Types include:

  • user — facts about you (background, preferences, constraints)
  • feedback — how you want Coop to behave or adjust (corrections, tone)
  • project — specific things you're working on
  • reference — stories, anecdotes, evidence (often produced by the gap → story loop)

How it gets used

When Coop builds the system prompt for a new message, buildCoopMemoryBlock() selects relevant memories and injects them into the context. You see this surface as Coop "remembering" things from previous sessions.

Where to manage it

Career OS > Coop > Memory shows the typed entries. You can delete anything that's wrong or stale. The memory model used for synthesis is configurable separately from the chat model.

Operating Principles

A user-editable textarea in Career OS that defines the rules Coop uses to interpret dealbreakers, calibrate scores, and shape draft tone. This replaces roughly twenty hardcoded opinions that used to live in code.

Where

Career OS > Coop > Operating Principles. The textarea ships with a sensible default. Edit it freely — whatever's there is what Coop will follow.

What goes in it

  • How to interpret your dealbreakers (e.g. "remote means fully remote, hybrid means co-located")
  • How to calibrate scores (e.g. "7+ is reserved for genuine fits, not pessimistic middle defaults")
  • Tone and length rules for drafts (e.g. cover letters, intro requests)
  • How to weigh title vs scope vs comp when they conflict
  • Anything else you want Coop to apply consistently

There's also a smaller Custom instructions textarea below it for one-off tweaks. Most rules belong in Operating Principles.

i Operating Principles are loaded into both chat context and the autofill/scoring system prompt. Editing them changes how Coop scores future opportunities, so consider re-scoring open roles after a substantive change.
Screen Share with Coop
New

Share your screen with Coop so he can see exactly what you're looking at. When enabled, Coop captures a screenshot of your active browser tab before each message and sends it to the AI model for visual analysis.

How to use

  1. Look for the monitor icon in Coop's chat header (between the title and pop-out button)
  2. Click it to enable — it turns coral when active
  3. A preview thumbnail appears above the input showing what Coop will see
  4. Send any message — Coop sees both your text and your screen
  5. Click the icon again to disable

What Coop sees

Coop receives a JPEG screenshot of the visible browser tab. He can read text, see layouts, identify form fields, and understand page structure. He cannot see videos, animations, or content behind login walls on other tabs.

Great for

  • Filling out application forms — "help me answer this question"
  • Reading job postings — "what do you think of this role?"
  • Email drafting — "help me respond to this"
  • Any page where context matters
💡 Cost: ~$0.0003-0.005 per message with screenshot depending on model. Only charged when you send a message, not for preview updates.
Qualification Matching
New

When Coop scores a job, he now extracts up to 10 specific qualifications from the posting and matches each one against your resume, skills, and experience.

✅
Qualification drawer auto-expanded showing met/partial/unmet items with evidence

How it works

Each qualification is classified as:

  • ✓ Met — clear match found in your background
  • ◐ Partial — related experience but not exact
  • ✗ Unmet — no evidence in your profile
  • ? Unknown — can't determine from available info

Each qualification also shows its importance level (required preferred bonus) and evidence from your background.

Where to find it

In the sidepanel job opportunity section, look for the collapsible "Qualifications (N/M met)" section below Green/Red Flags. Also visible on the Opportunity detail page.

"See why" inline expand

Each opportunity card has a See why toggle that expands the full qualification list inline — no modal — with status icon, importance, and Coop's evidence for each item.

Per-requirement corrections

For any qualification, you can tell Coop:

  • I meet this — flips to Met and writes the correction to learned insights
  • Not relevant — drops the requirement entirely from this role
  • Wrong evidence — tells Coop the supporting evidence he found doesn't apply, and asks for the right one

Re-score with corrections

After making one or more corrections, hit Re-score with corrections on the card. Coop re-runs scoring with your overrides applied, so you can immediately see the updated overall score and breakdown.

Curation

Click the × button on any qualification to dismiss it. Dismissed qualifications show as strikethrough and persist across sessions.

Score Breakdown
New

Every job match score includes a visual breakdown showing the five weighted dimensions. Each is scored 1-10 and displayed as a colored horizontal bar — green (7+), amber (4-6), red (1-3):

  • Qualification (20%) — deterministic from met/partial/unmet requirement counts
  • Role Fit (20%) — flag-based: role type, seniority, scope alignment
  • Culture Fit (25%) — flag-based: leadership style, remote culture, growth signals
  • Company Fit (20%) — firmographic signals: stage, industry, product-market fit
  • Comp Fit (15%) — base/OTE vs your floor and strong-offer thresholds

The weighted math row at the bottom of queue cards shows the exact formula: Q×20 + R×20 + Cu×25 + Co×20 + Comp×15 = X/100 → 1-10. Find the breakdown in the collapsible "Score Breakdown" section in the sidepanel, opportunity detail page, and Apply Queue cards.

Flag curation

Individual red flags can be dismissed in the score modal (Kanban card view). Click × on any red flag to mark it as wrong/overblown. Dismissed flags are excluded from re-scoring — Coop learns from your feedback.

Gradient colors

Green and red flags now use gradient colors — the most important signal is darkest, fading lighter for less critical ones. This gives you an instant visual sense of severity.

How Scoring Works
Updated

Coop uses a deterministic 5-dimension scoring model. The LLM detects which flags fired and extracts evidence; all scoring math happens in JavaScript — no black-box LLM scores.

📐
Queue card showing the 5-dimension score breakdown with weighted math row

The five dimensions

  • Qualification (20%) — Deterministic from met/partial/unmet counts. Formula: 10 × credits / total where met=1, partial=0.5, unmet=0. Comp-related qualifications are filtered out. The qualification drawer auto-expands so you always see what was matched.
  • Role Fit (20%) — Starts at 5.0 baseline, adjusted by green/red flag deltas. Each flag has a severity (1-5) and contributes ±severity × 0.5. Clamped to 1-10.
  • Culture Fit (25%) — Same flag-based formula. Detects signals like leadership style, remote culture, growth mindset from the posting and company context.
  • Company Fit (20%) — Uses firmographic data (employees, funding, industry, product) when available. Enrichment auto-fetches from company website/LinkedIn if not stored.
  • Comp Fit (15%) — Base and OTE compared against your floor and strong-offer thresholds. Flag-based adjustments for above/below signals.

The overall score

Weighted sum: Q×20 + R×20 + Cu×25 + Co×20 + Comp×15 = X/100 → 1-10. The math row at the bottom of every queue card shows the exact formula so you can verify the number.

Job description auto-fetch

If a job description isn't stored when scoring runs but a job URL exists, Coop automatically fetches the page, strips HTML to plain text, and persists it. This means opportunities saved from LinkedIn company pages (without visiting the actual posting) will still get full qualification extraction on first score.

Firmographic enrichment in scoring

The scoring prompt now receives structured company data — employees, funding, industry, product overview, target customers, and competitors — when available on the entry. Previously only unstructured web snippets were passed, leading to "no company data" even when research had been completed.

Qualification matching

Coop extracts all qualifications from the job posting — including implicit ones based on role level and company. Each is rated:

  • Met (✓) — Clear evidence in your profile/resume
  • Partial (◐) — Related experience but with a gap (e.g., "managed 5-person team; role needs 50+")
  • Unmet (✗) — No evidence in your background
  • Unknown (?) — Can't determine from available information

Click any qualification's status icon to override it and explain why. Coop learns from your corrections and applies them to future scoring.

Training Coop's scoring

Coop improves through your feedback. Every correction is stored as a high-priority learned insight:

  • Dismiss a flag — Click × on any red or green flag, optionally explain why. Coop won't repeat dismissed flags and learns from your reasoning.
  • Override a qualification — Click the status icon (✓/◐/✗) to change it. Add evidence from your background that Coop missed.
  • Rescore — After giving feedback, hit "Rescore" to see Coop's updated assessment.
  • Profile updates — Keep your Career OS profile current. Coop can only match qualifications against what he knows about you.

Hard disqualification (DQ)

A Hard DQ flags roles with absolute verified dealbreakers — on-site when you require remote, base salary max below your floor, or fundamentally wrong role type. These show a red warning banner on the queue card.

Auto-rescore

When enabled, profile changes trigger automatic rescoring. You control which pipeline stages get rescored — by default only Scoring Queue and Interested. This prevents burning API credits on roles you've already passed on. The toggle and stage selection live in Data Pipeline & Usage (not Career OS).

Cost Tracking
New

Coop tracks every paid API call — Claude, OpenAI, Apollo, Serper, and Granola — so you always know what your research and scoring are costing.

The cost pill

A persistent pill in the pipeline header shows your running session cost (e.g. $0.42). Click it to open the full Cost Breakdown modal.

📊
Cost pill in the pipeline header — click for full breakdown

Cost breakdown modal

The modal shows three views of your spending:

  • By Provider — color-coded bars for Claude, OpenAI, Apollo, Serper, Granola. Each shows request count, token usage, cost, and percentage of total spend.
  • By Model — sorted by cost descending. See which model (Haiku, GPT-4.1 mini, Sonnet, etc.) is driving spend.
  • Recent Calls — last 20 API calls with relative time, model name, token counts, and per-call cost.

What gets tracked

  • AI calls — every claudeApiCall() and openAiChatCall() logs provider, model, input/output tokens, and computed cost
  • Non-AI calls — Apollo, Serper, and Granola requests are logged as request-only entries (no token counts)
  • Retention — call log entries expire after 24 hours and cap at 200 entries
💡 All cost tracking is local. No usage data is sent to any server. The cost pill updates in real-time as you use the extension.

Data & Storage

All data is stored locally in your browser. Nothing is sent to any Coop.ai server.

Storage keys

  • savedCompanies — single array of all company/opportunity entries (chrome.storage.local). Opportunities are entries with isOpportunity: true.
  • researchCache — research results keyed by normalized company name, 24h TTL (chrome.storage.local)
  • coopMemory — typed long-term memory entries used to enrich Coop's system prompt (chrome.storage.local)
  • storyTime — raw input + summary + learned insights for the Story Time profile (chrome.storage.local)
  • opportunityStages / companyStages — custom pipeline stage configurations
  • stageCelebrations — per-stage confetti and sound overrides
  • prefs — your profile and job preferences, used for scoring (chrome.storage.sync, syncs across devices)
  • API keys — stored in chrome.storage.local via the Integrations page (not synced)
  • coopConfig — pipeline configuration: enrichment order, AI model assignments per task, auto-rescore settings, scoring preferences (chrome.storage.local)
  • gmailConnected — flag for Gmail connection state
  • granolaToken — Granola REST API key
  • ci_co_layout_[id] / ci_co_collapsed_[id] — per-company panel layout in localStorage
  • ci_tblCols — active Table view column set, persisted in localStorage
